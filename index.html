King of Oil and Hearts

The man who donated all his wealth for his own country

Here's a time line of Haji Zaynalabdin's life:

  • 1823 - Born on September 22 in the village of Taghiyev, Azerbaijan.
  • 1850s - Became involved in the burgeoning oil industry in the Baku region, laying the foundation for his future wealth and influence.
  • Late 19th century - Established and expanded his oil business, earning him the nickname "the King of Oil."
  • 1880 - Began actively supporting various charitable causes and philanthropic projects.
  • 1882 - Constructed the Taghiyev Water Supply, which brought clean water to Baku and significantly improved public health.
  • 1883 - Contributed to the construction of the Baku-Batumi railway, enhancing transportation and trade in the region.
  • 1896 - Donated funds for the establishment of the Baku Technical School (later known as Azerbaijan State Oil Academy), promoting education in engineering and sciences.
  • 1900 - Supported the construction of the Baku City Hospital, a major medical facility in the city.
  • 1905 - Funded the construction of the Taghiyev Mosque in Baku, a significant religious and architectural landmark.
  • Early 20th century - Continued his philanthropic efforts, contributing to the development of education, healthcare, and cultural projects in Azerbaijan and beyond.
  • 1924 - Passed away on August 26, leaving behind a legacy of philanthropy and development in Azerbaijan. His contributions are still remembered and celebrated in the country's history.
